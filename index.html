The problem with most algorithms books is that theyre too focused on being entirely correct in the strict, academic sense. The truth is this: all that math-y notation is useless. 


People don't remember concepts by remembering complicated formulas with Greek letters. Its much easier to build a mental model little by little, from examples such as if the solution requires iterating through an input list, then the runtime is linear - O(n) where n is the size of the list. 


Once you see all the 20 or so pertinent mental models, you will effortlessly understand the time complexity of any algorithm. It will be a matter of composing the time complexities of the examples you already know.
